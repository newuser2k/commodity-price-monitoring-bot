# AI and Automation Angle

This document explains how the Commodity Price Monitoring & Alerting Bot can be positioned as a practical automation case study for business workflows.

The main idea is not to replace business decision-making, but to improve visibility, structure, and reaction time for procurement, operations, supply chain, and commercial teams.

## Why AI and Automation Matter

Many business processes still depend on manual monitoring, fragmented communication, and delayed updates.

Procurement, operations, and commercial teams often need to track external market signals such as commodity prices, supplier price changes, logistics cost indicators, FX rates, or market news.

Without automation, this process may be slow, inconsistent, and difficult to document.

A lightweight automation system can help teams receive structured updates, reduce manual monitoring, and focus on decisions instead of repetitive checking.

## Automation Role

In this case study, automation can support the workflow by:

* Monitoring market data
* Evaluating price movements against predefined rules
* Classifying relevant events
* Logging structured records
* Sending Telegram notifications
* Generating daily or intraday summaries
* Supporting management visibility

The automation layer does not make final business decisions. It prepares structured information for human review.

## Possible AI Role

AI can be added as an optional layer to improve the quality of business communication and analysis.

Possible AI-assisted functions:

* Summarizing market movements in business-friendly language
* Converting raw events into management summaries
* Explaining why a price movement may be relevant for procurement or operations
* Classifying alerts by business impact
* Preparing supplier negotiation notes
* Drafting procurement review comments
* Creating weekly management summaries
* Highlighting repeated patterns or recurring alerts

## Example AI-Assisted Summary

```text
Brent oil moved moderately higher during the reporting period. The movement does not require immediate escalation, but it may be relevant for procurement teams reviewing supplier quotations, logistics-heavy categories, or commercial pricing assumptions. Continued monitoring is recommended if the upward movement continues.
```

## Human-in-the-Loop Principle

The system should remain human-in-the-loop.

AI can help structure and summarize information, but procurement, operations, commercial, or management teams should make the final decisions.

This is important because business context may include factors that the system does not fully understand, such as:

* Supplier relationships
* Contract terms
* Urgency of the purchase
* Inventory availability
* Customer commitments
* Regional constraints
* Internal approval processes

## Business Value of AI-Assisted Automation

The value of AI-assisted automation is practical and operational.

It can help teams:

* Reduce repetitive monitoring
* Improve clarity of communication
* Create structured summaries
* Save management time
* Support faster review of market events
* Improve documentation of alerts and decisions
* Make external market signals easier to understand for non-specialist users

## Positioning

This project should be positioned as:

**Business workflow automation for commodity price monitoring, alerts, reporting, and decision support.**

It should not be positioned as a trading product, financial advice tool, or investment recommendation system.

## Summary

AI and automation can be useful when they are connected to real business workflows.

In this case study, the technology supports procurement, operations, supply chain, and commercial teams by turning external market movements into structured alerts, summaries, and decision-support information.
