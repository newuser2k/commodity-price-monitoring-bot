# Business Context

Commodity price movements can have a direct impact on procurement, supply chain, logistics, and commercial operations.

For companies working with industrial products, energy-related equipment, MRO supplies, technical goods, or logistics-heavy operations, changes in oil and other commodity prices may influence:

* Supplier quotations
* Procurement timing
* Contract discussions
* Logistics and freight costs
* Inventory planning
* Commercial pricing
* Margin control
* Customer communication
* Management reporting

In many organizations, monitoring external market signals is still a manual process. Managers may check prices across different sources, forward updates manually, or react only after a market movement has already affected quotations or costs.

The purpose of this case study is to show how a lightweight automation layer can help business teams receive structured price alerts and market status updates through a simple communication channel such as Telegram.

## Business Problem

Procurement, operations, and commercial teams often need to react to changing market conditions, but they may not have a dedicated monitoring system for commodity-driven risks.

Typical problems include:

* Manual price checking
* Delayed awareness of market movements
* Fragmented communication between teams
* No structured event history
* Limited visibility for managers
* Missed timing opportunities in procurement or supplier negotiations
* Slow reaction to external cost drivers

## Proposed Solution

A Telegram-based commodity price monitoring bot can provide structured alerts and updates when important price movements are detected.

Instead of relying on manual checks, the system can monitor Brent oil price movements, evaluate market changes against predefined conditions, log relevant events, and send concise notifications to users.

## Target Users

This type of tool can be useful for:

* Procurement managers
* Supply chain managers
* Operations managers
* Commercial managers
* Business development managers
* Logistics coordinators
* Management teams
* Small businesses exposed to commodity-driven cost changes

## Business Value

The main value of the system is not prediction. The value is faster visibility, better structure, and improved reaction time.

The system can help teams:

* Reduce manual monitoring
* Receive timely alerts
* Improve procurement preparation
* Support supplier negotiation timing
* Track relevant market events
* Improve management visibility
* Build a structured history of important price movements
* Support better commercial and operational decision-making
