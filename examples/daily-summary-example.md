# Daily Summary Example

This document shows an example daily summary that could be generated by the Commodity Price Monitoring & Alerting Bot.

The purpose of the daily summary is to provide procurement, operations, commercial, and management teams with a concise view of relevant commodity price movements during the reporting period.

## Example Daily Summary

```text
📊 Daily Commodity Price Summary

Commodity: Brent oil
Reporting period: 09:00 - 18:00
Current price: 82.45 USD/bbl
Opening reference: 81.90 USD/bbl
Daily high: 83.10 USD/bbl
Daily low: 81.70 USD/bbl
Net change: +0.55 USD / +0.67%

Market movement:
Brent showed moderate upward movement during the reporting period, with the price staying within a relatively controlled intraday range.

Key events:
- Price moved above the opening reference level
- Intraday high reached 83.10 USD/bbl
- No major volatility alert was triggered
- No immediate management escalation required

Business relevance:
The movement may be relevant for procurement timing, supplier quotation review, logistics cost awareness, and commercial planning.

Suggested review:
- Monitor whether suppliers adjust quotations in response to the price movement
- Review open procurement requests where commodity-linked cost exposure may be relevant
- Keep commercial teams informed if the movement continues
```

## Management Version

```text
📌 Management Commodity Update

Brent oil closed the reporting period at 82.45 USD/bbl, up 0.67% from the opening reference.

The movement was moderate and did not trigger a major volatility alert.

Business impact:
No immediate escalation is required, but procurement and commercial teams should continue monitoring supplier quotations and cost assumptions.

Recommended action:
Continue observation and review open procurement cases if the upward movement continues.
```

## Procurement Version

```text
🧾 Procurement Review Note

Commodity: Brent oil
Current price: 82.45 USD/bbl
Daily change: +0.67%

Procurement relevance:
The moderate upward movement may affect supplier pricing assumptions for energy-related, logistics-heavy, or commodity-sensitive product categories.

Suggested procurement actions:
- Review open supplier quotations
- Monitor pending RFQs
- Check whether upcoming purchases may be exposed to commodity-driven cost changes
- Keep track of supplier price validity periods
```

## Commercial Version

```text
💼 Commercial Operations Note

Brent oil moved moderately higher during the reporting period.

Commercial relevance:
The movement may be relevant for pricing discussions, margin awareness, customer communication, and contract timing.

Suggested review:
- Check whether open customer quotations include commodity-sensitive cost assumptions
- Monitor margin exposure on active opportunities
- Inform commercial teams if the upward movement continues
```

## Summary Design Principles

A useful daily summary should be:

* Short
* Structured
* Business-friendly
* Action-oriented
* Easy to forward to management or team members
* Focused on business relevance, not raw market data

## Notes

The examples are illustrative and use sample values only.
