# Telegram Alert Example

This document shows example Telegram notifications that could be generated by the Commodity Price Monitoring & Alerting Bot.

The messages are written in a business-friendly format so that procurement, operations, and commercial teams can quickly understand the market movement and decide whether any action is needed.

## Example 1 — Price Increase Alert

```text
🔔 Brent Price Alert

Current price: 84.25 USD/bbl
Previous update: 82.90 USD/bbl
Change: +1.35 USD / +1.63%

Event type: Price increase
Reason: Brent price moved above the configured intraday threshold.

Business note:
This movement may be relevant for procurement timing, supplier quotation review, and commercial cost planning.

Suggested review:
Check active supplier quotations and monitor whether the movement continues.
```

## Example 2 — Price Decrease Alert

```text
🔔 Brent Price Alert

Current price: 79.80 USD/bbl
Previous update: 81.10 USD/bbl
Change: -1.30 USD / -1.60%

Event type: Price decrease
Reason: Brent price moved below the configured intraday threshold.

Business note:
This movement may create an opportunity to review procurement timing or supplier pricing assumptions.

Suggested review:
Check upcoming purchasing decisions and compare current supplier quotations with recent market movement.
```

## Example 3 — Daily Status Update

```text
📊 Brent Daily Status Update

Current price: 82.45 USD/bbl
Daily range: 81.70 - 83.10 USD/bbl
Daily change: +0.55 USD / +0.67%

Market status:
Moderate upward movement during the day.

Business note:
No immediate escalation required, but procurement and commercial teams may want to keep monitoring supplier price reactions.
```

## Example 4 — Management Summary

```text
📌 Commodity Market Summary

Commodity: Brent oil
Current price: 82.45 USD/bbl
Key movement: +2.10% over the last reporting period

Relevant business areas:
- Procurement
- Supplier negotiations
- Logistics cost awareness
- Commercial pricing
- Margin control

Summary:
Brent moved higher during the reporting period. The movement may be relevant for teams managing industrial supply, energy-related products, MRO categories, or logistics-heavy operations.

Recommended action:
Review open supplier quotations and monitor whether the movement affects upcoming procurement or commercial discussions.
```

## Design Principles

The notifications should be:

* Short enough to read quickly
* Clear enough for non-technical users
* Structured for business review
* Focused on action, not raw market data
* Suitable for procurement, operations, and management teams

## Notes

The examples are illustrative and use sample values only.

This project is presented as a business automation and market monitoring case study. It is not financial advice, not an investment recommendation, and not a trading product.
